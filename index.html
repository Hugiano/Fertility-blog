What is Infertility?

According to the World Health Organization, Infertility is a disease of the male or female reproductive system defined by the failure to achieve a pregnancy after 12 months or more of regular unprotected sexual intercourse.

Infertility affects millions of people of reproductive age worldwide – and has an impact on their families and communities. Estimates suggest that between 48 million couples and 186 million individuals live with infertility globally.

it is important to note that the word infertility used above is merely a description of a disease condition and not a parmanent state and just like most disease conditions, it can be treated with great success. see treatment

female Infertility?

Female infertility occurs when a woman is unable to get pregnant because of underlying medical conditions/problems

How common is female infertility?

Infertility is a common disease. At least 10% of women deal with infertility of some kind. The chances of being infertile increases as a woman ages..
