What is Infertility?

According to the World Health Organization, Infertility is a disease of the male or female reproductive system defined by the failure to achieve a pregnancy after 12 months or more of regular unprotected sexual intercourse.

Infertility affects millions of people of reproductive age worldwide – and has an impact on their families and communities. Estimates suggest that between 48 million couples and 186 million individuals live with infertility globally.

it is important to note that the word infertility used above is merely a description of a disease condition and not a parmanent state and just like most disease conditions, it can be treated with great success. see treatment

female Infertility?

Female infertility occurs when a woman is unable to get pregnant because of underlying medical conditions/problems

How common is female infertility?

Infertility is a common disease. At least 10% of women deal with infertility of some kind. The chances of being infertile increases as a woman ages..

Causes of Infertility?

The difficulties of getting pregnant most often comes with aging and many age related challenges such as: abnormal development of oocyte (eggs); DNA damage; endometriosis etc. This is problems/challenges arise because as we get old, many bodily functions begin to decline. This decline is caused by a drop the bodies NAD+ levels. simply put, "As you get old, your NAD+ levels decline. As your NAD+ levels decline, your body becomes less capable of taking care of its needs."

Therefore reducing or supplementing depleting NAD+ levels is an esential step to preventing age related female infertility.

Treatment

Now we know that depleting NAD+ levels is responsible for malformation of maternal oocyte, DNA damage and subsequently infertility.


Therefore, supplementing depleting NAD+ levels will effectively improve the quality of maternally aged oocytes and therefore increase the chances of fertility


What is NAD+?

NAD+ (Nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide) is an important cofactor in all living cells that is involved in fundamental biological processes like metabolism, cell signalling, gene expression, and DNA repair.


NAD+ basically plays many essential roles in you bodies metabolism. it is like the building block for every biological process that takes place in the body.
